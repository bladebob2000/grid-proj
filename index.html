The Ethical Core of Comedy

Is Dark Humor Morally Permissible?

Humor can help us cope — but it can also cause harm. This project explores where moral boundaries form through major ethical frameworks.

Consequences

Utilitarianism

Mill, John Stuart. Utilitarianism. 1863.

Utilitarianism evaluates morality by outcomes — specifically whether an action increases happiness or suffering. Dark humor is morally permissible when it creates relief or connection without causing meaningful harm.

However, when jokes inflict emotional pain or reduce well-being, they fail the utilitarian test. Ultimately, this framework asks a simple question: does the laughter outweigh the pain?

Dignity & Duty

Kantian Ethics

Johnson & Cureton. Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy.

Kantian ethics focuses on respect for human dignity. Humor becomes immoral when it treats people as objects for entertainment rather than autonomous individuals.

Dark humor may be permissible if it respects humanity — for example, by mocking injustice rather than exploiting suffering.

Personal Feeling

Ethical Subjectivism

Rachels, James. “Subjectivism in Ethics.”

Ethical Subjectivism claims moral judgments reflect personal attitudes rather than objective truths. Under this view, a joke is wrong only if someone feels it is wrong.

Rachels criticizes this position, noting that it cannot explain meaningful moral disagreement or justify criticism of harmful humor.

The Human Mind

Psychological Impact

Brigaud & Blanc. National Library of Medicine.

Research suggests dark humor can reduce emotional resistance to moral violations, making harmful actions feel less severe.

While this can aid coping, it also risks desensitizing individuals to real suffering — raising ethical concerns.

Coping & Truth

In Defense of Dark Humor

Bills, Rebekah. Providence.

In high-stress professions, dark humor can be a survival tool. Rather than trivializing suffering, it allows people to confront harsh realities without emotional collapse.

When used to cope or reveal truth — rather than demean — dark humor may be morally valuable.

The Verdict

Dark humor is neither inherently moral nor immoral. Its permissibility depends on intent, context, and impact.

Used to connect or cope, it can be ethically justified. Used to exploit or dehumanize, it becomes morally wrong.
