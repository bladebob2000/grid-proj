Ethical Lenses

These three frameworks judge dark humor differently — outcomes vs dignity vs personal feeling. Together, they show why context matters.

Utilitarianism

Morality = outcomes

Does the overall benefit outweigh the harm?

  • Focuses on happiness vs suffering as the moral score.
  • Dark humor can be acceptable if it creates relief and connection.
  • It becomes wrong when it causes real emotional damage or spreads harm.

A utilitarian would ask: does this joke increase well-being overall?

Optional visual: “happiness vs harm”

Kantian Ethics

Morality = respect for dignity

Are people being treated as ends, not objects?

  • Intent and respect matter, not just reactions and laughter.
  • Humor is wrong if it uses someone’s pain as entertainment.
  • More defensible when it targets injustice rather than victims.

A Kantian would ask: does this joke respect human dignity?

Optional visual: “dignity / autonomy”

Ethical Subjectivism

Morality = personal reaction

Is “wrong” just “I dislike it”?

  • Explains why people disagree: they feel differently.
  • Dark humor can be “fine” for one person and “wrong” for another.
  • The problem: it’s hard to set limits when harm affects others.

A subjectivist lens asks: how do different people experience this joke?
